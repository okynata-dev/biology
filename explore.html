Trait Explorer.

Every Biom is built from a finite vocabulary of biological traits — cell shapes, staining systems, organelles, lifecycle states. Below, each trait is isolated so you can see what it looks like on its own.

11 traits

Morphology

The structural shape of the colony. Inherited from how cells divide and arrange themselves under the microscope.

12 traits

Staining

Each biom is "stained" with one of twelve laboratory staining systems — real protocols used in bacteriology to reveal cell wall chemistry.

10 traits

Organelles

Subcellular structures. Each appears independently with its own probability — a single biom may show several organelles, or only the always-present capsule.

6 traits · mutually exclusive

Reserve Granules

Cellular cargo — what the biom stores inside. Each biom carries one type of reserve, or none at all.

4 traits · mutually exclusive

Lifecycle State

The moment in the cell's life this biom is caught in — feeding, dividing, sporulating, or fixing nitrogen.

3 traits · independent · rare

Ultra-rare Effects

Anomalies. Each appears in roughly one to two percent of bioms. A few specimens carry more than one.

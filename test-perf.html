Heavy vs Lite — performance test

Same four bioms, rendered both ways. Scroll this page up & down, tab away and come back, drag the window around. Look at the iframes — they should LOOK near-identical, but the right column should feel noticeably more responsive (especially on a hot Mac).

Heavy — current prod unchanged
Border-radius morphs ±8 % on 8 corner radii of every panel, 30 fps. Each morph re-rasterises the clip shape and forces backdrop-filter to re-blur. 15 panels × 30 fps × 8-radius compute × blur is most of the cost.
Lite — 4 free wins optimised
Border-radius morph disabled (panels still drift, but their outlines hold steady) + contain: paint on each panel + will-change: transform for GPU layer promotion. Visually a panel's shape is frozen but everything else — drift, colour, glass, halo — identical.
What to look for:
  1. Visual diff: stare at one heavy biom and one lite biom side-by-side. Pause for 10 seconds. Notice the heavy one's outline gently wobbles, the lite one stays still. That's the only intentional difference. Everything else — glass, drift, accents, halo — is identical.
  2. Scroll feel: scroll the page rapidly up & down. On a stressed Mac the heavy column should hitch / stutter more. Lite column should glide.
  3. Hover / drag: select-drag some text across the page, hover over UI elements. Heavy column triggers more repaint of the bioms behind it.
  4. Tab switch: cmd+tab away for 5 seconds, come back. Look at how fast each side re-animates.

If lite looks indistinguishable while feeling lighter — green-light to apply this everywhere (OpenSea animation_url, landing hero iframes).
